This glossary contains important terms and concepts discussed in Networking. Understanding them will help solidify and deepen your understanding.

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
|  Networking | the process of connecting two or more computing devices (like computers, phones, servers, or even smart devices) so they can exchange data with each other.
| Security Groups | virtual firewalls for your instances (like EC2 virtual servers) to control inbound (incoming) and outbound (outgoing) traffic.
| Isolation | Resources for different applications, customers, or environments can be kept completely separate, preventing interference and enhancing security.
| Performance | An efficient network design leads to faster application response times and a better experience for your users.
| Virtual Private Cloud (VPC) | your own isolated, virtual network within the AWS cloud.
| IP Address Ranges | You define the private IP address range for your VPC (e.g., 10.0.0.0/16), which acts as the foundation for all the private addresses within your network.
| Route Tables | Determine where network traffic from your subnets is directed – either within your VPC, to the internet, or to other connected networks.
| Inbound Rules | Dictate which traffic is allowed into your instances.
| Outbound Rules | Control the traffic leaving your instance.
| CloudFront (Global Edge Network) | CloudFront is a global content delivery network (CDN).
| S3 (Static Website Hosting) | An object storage service used here to host your frontend website.
| VPC (Virtual Private Cloud) | Your own private network inside AWS.
| Internet Gateway (IGW) | A door between your VPC and the public internet.
| RDS (PostgreSQL Database) | A managed database service.
| Subnets | smaller divisions of your VPC's IP range. (A physically isolated location within a region), allowing for high availability.
| DB Subnet Group | A list of subnets where RDS is allowed to run.
| CloudFront (Global Edge Network) | CloudFront is a global content delivery network (CDN).
