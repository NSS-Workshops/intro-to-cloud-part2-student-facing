This glossary contains important terms and concepts discussed in IAM Basics and Permissions. Understanding them will help solidify and deepen your understanding.

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| Identity and Access Management (IAM) | IAM is the security foundation of cloud infrastructure. IAM allows you to control Authentication, Authorization,and Conditions.
| Identities | the digital representations of "who" or "what" is requesting access to resources, including users (humans), applications/services (workloads), or devices.
| Users | Users represent individual people or services that need to interact with your cloud environment.
| Roles | Roles are identities that can be assumed temporarily.
| Policies | Policies are JSON documents that define permissions. They answer: "What actions are allowed or denied on which resources?"
| Manged Policies | Pre packaged policies by AWS.
| Inline Policies | Custom policies written in json.
| The Principle of Least Privilege | Requiring that users, systems, or processes be granted only the minimum access rights—privileges—necessary to perform their specific, authorized functions.
