This glossary contains important terms and concepts discussed in Load Balancing Fundamentals. Understanding them will help solidify and deepen your understanding.

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| Load Balancing | Like a traffic cop for your application. It distributes incoming network traffic across multiple servers, ensuring no single server becomes a bottleneck. Helping your application handle a high volume of requests efficiently and reliably.
| Vertical Scaling (Scaling Up) | Increasing the resources of a single server
| Horizontal Scaling (Scaling Out) | Adding more servers to your existing infrastructure. Instead of making one server more powerful, you add more servers that work together to handle the load.
| Target Groups | Logical grouping of servers (or other resources) that a load balancer distributes traffic to.
| Health Check Endpoints | Specific URLs or ports on your application servers that the load balancer periodically checks to determine if a target is healthy and able to receive traffic.
